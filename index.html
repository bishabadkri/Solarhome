Quick facts and friendly guides about our Solar System
Explore! Explore! Explore!!

SUN

The Sun is a massive ball of hot gases and the center of our solar system.

Key Facts
  • AGE: About 4.6 billion years
  • Temperature: ~5,500°C on the surface
  • Fun Fact: The Sun contains 99.8% of the solar system’s total mass

EARTH

The only blue planet we know that supports life.

Key Facts
  • AGE: About 4.5 billion years
  • Distance from Sun: ~150 million km
  • Moon: 1 natural satellite
  • Fun Fact: 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by water

Dwarf Planet

Dwarf planets orbit the Sun but are not dominant in their orbit.

Key Facts
  • They are round but not dominant in their orbit
  • Location: Mostly in the Kuiper Belt
  • Fun Fact: Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006

ASTEROID

Asteroids are rocky objects left over from the early formation of our solar system.

Key Facts
  • Main Location: Asteroid Belt
  • Size: From a few meters to hundreds of kilometers
  • Largest Asteroid: Ceres (also a dwarf planet)
  • Fun Fact: Some asteroids may contain valuable metals

KUIPER BELT

The Kuiper Belt is a vast ring of icy bodies beyond Neptune.

Key Facts
  • Location: Starts about 30 AU from the Sun
  • Shape: Donut-shaped region
  • Contains: Ice, rock, and frozen gases
  • Fun Fact: It's like a "second asteroid belt," but much farther out

SPACE PROBES

Space probes are robotic spacecraft that explore space beyond Earth’s orbit.

Key Facts
  • Famous Probes: Voyager 1 & 2, New Horizons, Juno
  • No humans onboard: fully robotic
  • Power: Usually solar or nuclear
  • Fun Fact: Voyager 1 is the farthest human-made object in space

SATELLITE

A satellite is any object that orbits a planet. Some are natural; others are man-made for communication and observation.

Key Facts
  • Uses: Weather, GPS, communication, imaging
  • Fun Fact: Thousands of artificial satellites orbit Earth today
